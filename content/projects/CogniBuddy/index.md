With the advent of smart phones and algorithm based games young children aged 3 to 7 are increasingly becoming addicted to mobile phones and video games, this leads to reduced attention span, poor focus, and limited cognitive development. Since most mobile games prioritize entertainment and passive stimulation over meaningful learning, it makes it harder for children to engage in educational and productive activities. I propose an educational gaming module designed specifically for young children which combines fun gameplay with learning activities to improve focus and attention span.
 
The module can use age-appropriate challenges, controlled reward systems to promote healthy screen habits, turning screen time into a productive and engaging learning experience while avoiding the adverse effects of using a mobile screen. 

The basic games which can be added into the module is:

* Reflex training.

* Counting numbers.

* Interactive story reading.

* Addition,Substraction and Basic Arithmetic operators.

Initially developed as hobby project for reflex training, the project was later developed to make a socially impactful product by including more features. This was presented as part of my project for IPR in the second semester of KTU BTech. in EEE course.
